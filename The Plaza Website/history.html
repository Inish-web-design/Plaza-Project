Our History

A Legacy of Community Spirit

The Small Hall (1942)

During World War II, Buncrana's unique position near the border presented both challenges and opportunities. Forward-thinking community members recognized the potential of cross-border trade and social connections. The small hall was built as a gathering place where people from both sides of the border could meet, trade, and celebrate together safely. It quickly became a symbol of unity during uncertain times.

The Large Hall (1958)

Construction 1958

Built for the big band era

Sprung Oak Floor

Specially designed for dancing

The post-war economic boom and the golden age of big band music inspired the community to dream bigger. In 1958, construction began on the large hall to accommodate the era's popular big band performances and larger community events. The hall featured a specially designed sprung oak floor - perfect for dancing to the sounds of touring orchestras and local bands that defined the late 1950s social scene.

Through the 1960s-2000s, both halls served as the cultural heartbeat of Buncrana, hosting everything from wedding receptions and political rallies to amateur dramatics and charity fundraisers. Generations of families have celebrated life's milestones within these walls.

Recent Challenges and Renewal

Floor Challenges

Significant damage to oak floor

Community Response

Local residents rally for renovation

Time and use took their toll on our beloved facilities. The large hall's beautiful oak floor suffered significant damage, with only 50% salvageable despite restoration efforts. Rather than abandon this community treasure, local residents rallied together to fund a complete renovation project.

Today, The Plaza Project stands as a testament to what communities can achieve when they work together towards a shared vision.
